Have you ever looked at your comfortable, predictable routine and felt a sudden urge to light a match just to see what happens? I certainly have. There is a very specific type of literary fiction that scratches this exact itch: stories about people who accidentally blow up their safe, sensible lives and have to figure out what comes next. 

These aren't tales of grand, premeditated heists or calculated mid-life crises. These are books about messy humans who make one wrong turn, tell a catastrophic lie, or fail to keep up a harmless facade, resulting in their entire world unravelling. If you are sitting on your couch wondering what a total reset actually looks like—without having to risk your own credit score or marriage—here are eight books about characters who accidentally derail their safe lives to spark change.

## The Push by Ashley Audrain

Sly, quiet competence is the trap at the center of this psychological drama. Blythe Smart is determined to be the warm, attentive mother she never had, raising her daughter in a spotless house with a devoted husband. The problem is that she senses something deeply off about her child from infancy, while everyone around her—including her husband—believes she is just an anxious, unhinged narrator. Blythe's obsession with proving her instincts right tears down her marriage and her domestic peace brick by brick. It is a harrowing read about what happens when you stop ignoring your gut and let the truth burn your life to the ground.

## Fleishman Is in Trouble by Taffy Brodesser-Akner

Toby Fleishman thought he had successfully navigated the messy aftermath of a divorce by leaning into his newly single, app-enabled Manhattan dating life. He is the sensible doctor, the dedicated father, and the aggrieved ex-husband. Then, his ex-wife abruptly drops their two children off at his apartment days early and vanishes completely. Toby’s carefully constructed narrative of victimhood and routine crumbles as he is forced to parent alone and re-examine his own role in the marriage. It’s sharp, frantic, and brilliantly captures how a single unexpected crisis forces a total lifestyle audit.

## Where’d You Go, Bernadette by Maria Semple

Bernadette Fox is a former architectural genius who has retreated to a damp, falling-apart mansion in Seattle, dealing with her social anxiety by outsourcing her entire life to a virtual assistant in India. When her daughter Bee cashes in a promised family trip to Antarctica as a reward for good grades, Bernadette's fragile, hermetic bubble pops. To avoid the trip—and her aggressively normal suburban neighbors—Bernadette vanishes. It’s a hilarious, chaotic reminder of how easily our carefully curated hermit-lives can be upended by the people who love us most.

## Major Pettigrew’s Last Stand by Helen Simonson

Major Ernest Pettigrew is a retired widower living in a picturesque English village, defined entirely by his adherence to duty, proper tea manners, and the memory of his late wife. His safe world begins to tilt off its axis when he strikes up an unexpected friendship over a shared love of literature with Mrs. Ali, a local Pakistani shopkeeper. When he impulsively defends her family’s heirloom weapons from small-minded village gossip, he accidentally starts a local scandal that forces him to choose between a comfortable, isolated old age and a messy, genuine late-in-life romance. 

## Fates and Furies by Lauren Groff

On the surface, Lotto and Mathilde are the golden couple of the New York arts scene: married young, impossibly in love, and weathering early struggles to achieve glittering success. The first half of the book tells Lotto's version of this charmed life. Then, the second half flips the perspective to Mathilde, revealing that their entire foundation was built on a series of deliberate, massive omissions and hidden sacrifices. It is a masterclass in how a safe, idyllic partnership can be secretly propped up by a house of cards that is just waiting for a breeze.

## Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine by Gail Honeyman

Eleanor Oliphant’s life is governed by strict rules. She wears the same clothes, eats the exact same frozen meal for lunch every Friday, and talks to her horrific mother on the phone every Wednesday. She is lonely, but she is safe. That safety shatters the moment she and her awkward IT coworker, Raymond, witness an elderly man collapse on the street. By stopping to help him, Eleanor accidentally injects human warmth, chaos, and unpredictable friendship into her rigidly scheduled existence, forcing her to finally confront the childhood trauma she spent a decade avoiding.

## Standard Deviation by Katherine Heiny

Audrey is married to Graham, whose ex-wife, Elissa, is still bafflingly omnipresent in their lives, dropping by uninvited and generally lingering like a ghost at the feast. Audrey is unfailingly polite, patient, and determined to be the cool, modern second wife. But her polite facade wears paper-thin when Graham insists on hiring an eccentric, boundary-pushing woman with Asperger's to help in his office, completely throwing Audrey’s domestic equilibrium out of whack. It’s an exceptionally funny book about how trying to keep the peace can actually prevent you from living.

## The Vacationers by Emma Straub

Franny and Jim Post take their extended family and teenage children to Mallorca for a two-week summer vacation to celebrate their wedding anniversary. It is supposed to be a relaxing, high-end pause from their complicated lives back in New York, where Jim has just been fired from his high-profile job and disgraced by scandal. Instead of a peaceful Mediterranean reset, proximity and enforced leisure act like a pressure cooker. Secrets spill, long-held resentments flare up, and by the time they pack their bags to leave, not a single member of the family is returning to the exact life they left behind.

If you are trying to figure out which of these books to pick up first tonight, consider what kind of mess you are in the mood for. If you want a heartwarming, character-driven journey of tiny shifts, start with *Eleanor Oliphant*. If you want a witty, upper-middle-class explosion, grab *The Vacationers*.
